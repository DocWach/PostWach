# Two-Lens Evaluation

The codified method for the principal's standing directive (2026-07-17): every WySE / Hive-Empire candidate, result, seed, or scoping decision is scored on **two lenses, always**. This skill is the METHOD; the `two-lens-critic` agent is the independent GATE (methods = skills, gates = agents).

## Why this exists (the failure it prevents)

On 2026-07-17 the two lenses lived only in passive memory. The assistant scored F34 (WySE + Monte Carlo) on lens 1, found its novelty pre-empted (Michel-Siegle / Geiger), and **shelved it** -- missing the lens-2 harness win the principal was pointing at (ParaDEVS: harness the homomorphism-collapse, deliver a stratified measured version). Passive intent did not force practice. This skill makes the discipline **invokable**; the critic agent makes it **independently enforced**. Lesson encoded: *when lens-1 novelty looks pre-empted, do NOT shelve -- switch to lens 2 and HUNT the harness win.*

## When to load this skill

1. Scoping a seed or candidate ("is this Fable-worthy / worth building?").
2. Selecting what to run / prioritize next.
3. **Any decision to shelve, retire, or downgrade** a seed/candidate/result (load is MANDATORY here).
4. Framing how a finished result is presented (does it have a demonstrable in-hand win?).

Do NOT load it for pure mechanical execution (running a witness, a file op) or when the carry-gate alone suffices and no shelve/retire/prioritization decision is in play.

## The two lenses

**Lens 1 -- Research novelty & advancement.** Does the delta genuinely advance the field? The carry-vs-relabel gate. This is already codified in `morphism-research-methodology` (the "does the delta carry?" test); REFERENCE it, do not duplicate. A relabel (applies WySE to a domain without contributing to WySE's own theory) fails lens 1.

**Lens 2 -- The Steve Jobs iPhone / harness moment (official framing).** Principal, verbatim: *"Steve Jobs did not invent the touch screen, and, rather, he harnessed the touch screen and put it into the palm of our hands which revolutionized the tech industry."* So lens 2 = **HARNESS** an existing technology (not invent it) + **PUT IT IN THE PALM OF THE HAND** (accessible, usable, in-hand delivery) + thereby **REVOLUTIONIZE** the field.

**Load-bearing corollary.** Lens 2 can carry HIGH value even where lens-1 component-novelty is MODEST -- the iPhone's parts were not new; the harnessing + delivery was the revolution. This is exactly WySE's situation (a positioned-integration that harnesses Wymore / DEVS / Cody / entropy). **Do not undervalue a result because its components are ceded; ask whether the harnessing + delivery is transformative.**

## Procedure

1. **Lens 1 -- carry.** Apply the `morphism-research-methodology` carry-gate. State: what is the delta, does it carry or relabel, and what is the closest prior art (a prior-art check is often required, not optional).
2. **Lens 2 -- harness hunt (MANDATORY, even/especially when lens 1 looks weak).** Ask explicitly: *what existing technology/result can WySE HARNESS here, and what transformative in-hand delivery does WySE add?* If lens 1 came back "pre-empted," this step is the one that must not be skipped. Name the harnessed thing and the delivered win, or state honestly that none was found after a genuine hunt.
3. **Elegance check.** Is the solution AND the explanation elegant (E=mc^2 / LEGO standard)? A missing demonstrable, in-hand win is a GAP to close, not an afterthought. (Ties `project_wyse_vv_lego_elegance`.)
4. **Verdict.** Emit the structured output below. **A shelve / retire verdict is INVALID unless step 2 is documented** (what harness win was hunted for and why none was found).

## Output format

```
TWO-LENS VERDICT: <candidate/seed/result id>
Lens 1 (novelty/carry): CARRIES | RELABELS | PRE-EMPTED  -- <delta + closest prior art>
Lens 2 (harness/iPhone): HARNESS-WIN <name it> | NONE-FOUND-AFTER-HUNT  -- <what is harnessed + the in-hand delivery>
Elegance: <solution + explanation elegant? gap?>
Recommendation: RUN | SCOPE-FURTHER | REFRAME | SHELVE  -- <one line>
  (SHELVE requires Lens 2 = NONE-FOUND-AFTER-HUNT, documented)
```
