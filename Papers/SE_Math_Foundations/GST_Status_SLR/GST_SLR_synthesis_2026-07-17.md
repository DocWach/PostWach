## 0. WHITE adjudication summary (the load-bearing verdict)

The working hypothesis pre-registered in the protocol (§2) was: *no formal disproof of GST exists; the informal program was validly critiqued as vague/unproductive and superseded by formalization; a specific biological model may have been superseded; the rigorous core is alive.* WHITE was required to determine whether any RED finding **defeats** that hypothesis.

**Verdict: no RED finding defeats the working hypothesis. One RED finding sharpens it.**

The RED leg was tasked to build the strongest possible case that "GST was disproven" and reported `canBuildGenuineDisproof = false`, converging independently on the same buckets Phase-1 reported. RED did sharpen exactly one item to full empirical strength: the **metabolic-scaling** case is a *genuine empirical falsification* — but of a *specific universal biological law* (a single universal metabolic scaling exponent) that was attached to the GST-era organismic program, **not** of GST as a framework. WHITE accepts this as a real, adjudicated defeater of a narrow biological over-generalization and classifies it under RQ2 as **supersession of a specific model**, not disproof of the framework. This is the honest edge of the finding and it is stated as such below.

The distinction that carries the entire review: **a framework/paradigm is not the kind of object that gets "disproven"** in the Popperian sense; specific hypotheses inside or adjacent to it are. Every located hit that sounds like "disproof" is, on inspection, one of: critique-of-program (a-priori/methodological), institutional-decline (sociology of a research program), or a superseded specific model. None is a genuine empirical refutation of GST-the-framework, and no primary source using *disproven / refuted / falsified* against GST-the-framework was located.

## RQ1 — Provenance of the "disproven" claim

**Answer.** No documented **genuine disproof** of General Systems Theory exists in either the peer-reviewed or the grey literature. The "GST was disproven" claim, as a claim about the framework, is best explained as **misattribution**: real and often harsh critiques of the program's coherence, rigor, and productivity have been colloquially compressed into the word "disproven." Every located hit classifies as *critique-of-program*, *institutional-decline*, or (for RQ2) *superseded-specific-model*. No primary source uses "disproven/refuted/falsified" against GST-the-framework.

**Misattribution mechanism (the answer to the motivating conference remark).** The offhand "GST was disproven" most plausibly traces to colloquial compression of (a) Monod's "impossible/meaningless," (b) Berlinski's "sham," (c) Lilienfeld's "ideology," and (d) Shalizi-style "extinct" decline talk. A researcher hearing any of these secondhand can restate it as "disproven." That restatement is not supported by any primary source.

## RQ2 — Biology: any genuine falsification?

**Answer.** Yes — one, and it is narrow. A **universal** metabolic "surface law" / single universal metabolic scaling exponent (the "3/4-power law" read as a universal biological constant, and the "law of progressive reduction of metabolic rates") does **not** hold: allometric exponents vary across taxa and phenomena. This is a genuine EMPIRICAL falsification (RED sharpened this to full strength, citing the allometry literature — Glazier 2005 and fractal-network critiques **[UNVERIFIED-REF]**), and the 3/4-vs-2/3 dispute is *internal to allometry*.

**Crucial scoping — what it does and does not touch:**
- It falsifies a **specific universal biological law** that the organismic/GST-era program over-generalized. It does **not** touch GST-the-framework (open-system thermodynamics, equifinality, hierarchy).
- Bertalanffy's **own named growth model, the von Bertalanffy Growth Function (VBGF)**, has since 1957 been the most widely used growth model in fisheries and is still actively refined in the 2020s. So "his biology was disproven" is **false at the model level** — his signature model survives.
- Equifinality and the open-system framing remain standard vocabulary in developmental biology and systems biology; neither was refuted.

So the biological ledger is: one genuine supersession of a universal-law over-claim; the framework's core biological commitments and Bertalanffy's own model intact.

## RQ3 — Critiques: disproof vs supersession vs unproductivity

**Answer.** The major critiques (Berlinski 1976; Lilienfeld 1978; Monod 1974; Bunge 1977) are critiques of **ambition, rigor, and productivity** — not empirical refutations. They cluster into two non-disproof modes:

1. **Unproductivity / vagueness (the substantive, largely fair critique).** The informal 1950s grand-unification program (isomorphisms-across-all-disciplines) was legitimately criticized as vague and low-yield *as stated*. Berlinski's "sham"/"ceremonial" charge and Lilienfeld's "ideology" charge target the program's failure to convert grand analogy into working theory. Shalizi's institutional-decline observation is the sociological shadow of the same point. **This critique lands** — against the *informal program*, phase (a).

2. **A-priori impossibility (the self-undermining critique).** Monod's "impossible/meaningless" and Bunge's "nonscientific under both confirmationism and falsificationism" are philosophical, not empirical. Bunge's version notably backfires (he indicts the philosophy of science, not GST). Laszlo's pseudoscience anchor is stated *and rebutted in the same source*.

**The decisive move.** The valid unproductivity critique was answered not by defending the informal program but by **superseding it through formalization**. The vague "isomorphisms everywhere" claim became precise mathematical systems theory (see RQ4). So the correct reading of the critiques is: *the informal program was validly criticized as unproductive and was then superseded by its own formal descendants* — which is supersession, not disproof.

## RQ4 — Descendants: is the formal core alive?

**Answer.** Yes. The rigorous formal descendants of GST are mathematically well-defined, actively used, and productive. The formal core is a **living theory**.

- **Zeigler DEVS (Theory of Modeling and Simulation).** Rigorous discrete-event formalism; homomorphism/lumping theory (TMS ch. 22 stochastic homomorphism; ch. 15/17/21 core) is standard in M&S; actively developed and applied. Living. **[UNVERIFIED-REF for specific TMS 2018 edition]**
- **Wymore mathematical systems theory / WySE (1967, 1993).** Set-theoretic systems definition and the functional-to-buildable homomorphism; directly load-bearing in the portfolio's morphism library. Living (actively extended in the present program). **[UNVERIFIED-REF]**
- **Mesarovic–Takahara–Macko general systems theory.** Rigorous set-theoretic/abstract-systems formulation; a sibling lineage to Wymore's; underpins Cody's "systems theory of transfer learning." Living. **[UNVERIFIED-REF]**
- **Klir (architecture of systems problem solving).** Formal systems taxonomy; foundational and cited. **[UNVERIFIED-REF]**
- **T3SD** and Bayesian/decision-theoretic descendants — active in the portfolio.
- **External corroboration of vitality:** SEP's "Philosophy of Systems and Synthetic Biology" lists *(mathematical) general systems theory* as an active source of inspiration for minimal-life / systems-biology work — an independent, current, reputable signal that the formal core is not refuted but generative.

The formal descendants are precisely the productive answer to the RQ3 unproductivity critique: what the informal program gestured at as "isomorphisms across disciplines," DEVS/Wymore/Mesarovic make into provable morphisms between rigorously defined systems.

## RQ5 — Defensible STATUS statement

General Systems Theory was **not disproven.** No primary source in the peer-reviewed or grey literature refutes GST-the-framework; the "disproven" meme is a misattribution that compresses genuine critiques into a word none of their authors used. What actually happened separates cleanly into two phases:

- **(a) The informal grand-unification program** (von Bertalanffy's 1950s vision of isomorphisms uniting all sciences) was validly **criticized as vague and unproductive** *as stated* (Berlinski, Lilienfeld, Monod, Bunge) and **declined institutionally** (Shalizi). It was not falsified; it was **superseded** — largely by its own formalization. One adjacent specific biological over-claim (a universal metabolic scaling law) was genuinely **falsified**, but Bertalanffy's own growth model (VBGF) and the framework's core commitments (open systems, equifinality, hierarchy) survive.

- **(b) The rigorous formal core** — Wymore/WySE, Zeigler DEVS, Mesarovic–Takahara–Macko, Klir, and their Bayesian/decision-theoretic descendants — is **mathematically rigorous, active, and productive**: a **living theory**. The formal core is the productive fulfilment of what the informal program only gestured at.

**Positioning statement (deliverable):** *General Systems Theory was never disproven; a framework is not the kind of object that gets falsified. Its informal grand-unification program was fairly criticized as vague and unproductive and was superseded — not refuted — by formalization into mathematical systems theory. One specific biological over-generalization (a universal metabolic scaling law) was genuinely falsified, yet von Bertalanffy's own growth model and the framework's core concepts endure. The rigorous descendants that underpin this portfolio — Wymore/WySE, Zeigler's DEVS, Mesarovic–Takahara–Macko — constitute a living, productive formal core. The defensible claim is therefore not "GST survived intact," but the sharper and more useful one: the vague program was replaced by the exact theory it pointed toward, and building on that formal core is standing on live ground, not a refuted foundation.*
