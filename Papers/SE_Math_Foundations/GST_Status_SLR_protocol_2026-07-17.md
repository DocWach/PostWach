# SLR Protocol — "Was General Systems Theory disproven?" (provenance + formal-descendants positioning)

## 1. Background / rationale
An offhand conference remark ("GST was disproven"), never traceably sourced, motivates this review. GST (von Bertalanffy lineage) is the intellectual ROOT of the formal systems theories that underpin the portfolio (Wymore/WySE, Zeigler DEVS, Mesarovic-Takahara-Macko, Klir, T3SD). If the "disproven" meme circulates unexamined it undermines that foundation. **Gap:** no work systematically (a) traces the provenance of the "disproven" claim, nor (b) positions the rigorous formal descendants against it.

## 2. Objectives / research questions
- **RQ1 (provenance).** Does a documented "GST was disproven / refuted / falsified" claim exist in the scholarly OR grey literature? If so, WHO, WHAT precisely was claimed, and on WHAT basis? Classify each hit as: disproof-of-a-specific-hypothesis | critique-of-the-program | institutional-decline | misattribution.
- **RQ2 (biology).** Is any specific biological claim ORIGINATING in GST (von Bertalanffy: open-system thermodynamics of the organism, equifinality, allometric/organismic growth laws) genuinely FALSIFIED or superseded? Scope exactly what, and whether it touches GST-the-framework or only a specific model.
- **RQ3 (critique vs disproof).** What is the substance of the major CRITIQUES (Berlinski 1976 *On Systems Analysis*; Lilienfeld 1978 *The Rise of Systems Theory*; others), and do they amount to disproof, supersession, or unproductivity?
- **RQ4 (descendants).** How do the formal descendants (Wymore mathematical systems theory / WySE; Zeigler DEVS; Mesarovic-Takahara-Macko; Klir; T3SD) stand relative to the claim — are they rigorous, active, productive (i.e., does the formal core survive)?
- **RQ5 (synthesis).** State GST's defensible ACTUAL status, cleanly separating (a) the informal grand-unification program from (b) the rigorous formal core.

**Pre-registered working hypothesis (to be TESTED, not confirmed):** no formal disproof of GST exists; the informal 1950s program was validly critiqued as vague/unproductive AND superseded — precisely by formalization into DEVS/Wymore/Mesarovic; a specific biological model MAY have been superseded; the rigorous core is a living theory. **This review must genuinely STEELMAN the opposite** (that GST was disproven) before concluding.

## 3. Eligibility criteria
**Include:** peer-reviewed articles/books; foundational primary sources (Bertalanffy 1968 *General System Theory*; Mesarovic & Takahara; Wymore 1967/1993; Zeigler TMS; Klir); the critique works; AND any explicit "GST disproven/refuted/obsolete" claim in grey literature (textbooks, encyclopedias incl. SEP, lecture notes, reputable forums) — because an offhand conference claim likely lives OUTSIDE peer review, so RQ1 deliberately admits grey lit. Time: 1950–present. Language: English (+ seminal non-English by translation).
**Exclude:** pop-science with no argument; "systems theory" in unrelated senses (family-systems therapy, world-systems sociology) unless GST-derived; works merely USING systems methods without commenting on GST's status.

## 4. Information sources
Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore, JSTOR (older biology/philosophy), PhilPapers (philosophy of science), Google Scholar (forward/backward citation chaining from Bertalanffy 1968 + Berlinski 1976 + Lilienfeld 1978). Hand-search: ISSS (International Society for the Systems Sciences) proceedings, INCOSE, *Systems Research and Behavioral Science*. Grey lit: SEP, major systems-theory textbooks, curated lecture notes.

## 5. Search strategy (concept blocks)
- Block A (object): "general system* theory" OR "GST" OR Bertalanffy OR "systems theory".
- Block B (status): disproven OR refuted OR falsified OR debunked OR obsolete OR superseded OR "failure of" OR critique OR "is dead".
- Block C (descendants): Wymore OR Mesarovic OR "DEVS" OR Zeigler OR Klir OR "mathematical systems theory" OR formaliz*.
- RQ1/RQ3 = A AND B; RQ4 = A AND C. Citation-chain forward from Berlinski 1976 + Lilienfeld 1978.

## 6. Selection + bias control (mapped to our RBW discipline)
Two-stage screening (title/abstract → full text). Because a single agent screens, **run the review adversarially**: a RED leg actively hunts and STEELMANS the strongest case that "GST was disproven" (so we do not merely confirm our prior); a BLUE synthesis; a WHITE adjudication of whether any RED hit is a genuine disproof vs a critique/supersession. This is the `red-blue-white` skill applied to the SLR's bias-minimization requirement.

## 7. Extraction (per source)
Citation; claim about GST's status; the exact claim + its basis; RQ1 classification (disproof/critique/decline/misattribution); for descendants, the rigor/activity status; quality (Kitchenham SE guidelines / critical appraisal of the argument).

## 8. Synthesis
Narrative + thematic. Themes: (T1) the "disproven" claim's provenance — found / not-found / where; (T2) valid critiques (vagueness/unproductivity); (T3) genuine biological supersessions, if any; (T4) the formal descendants' living status. Evidence map (strong/moderate/limited/gap) per RQ.
