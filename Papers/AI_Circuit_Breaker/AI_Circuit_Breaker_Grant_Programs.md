# Grant Programs: Metrological Trust Underwriting for Autonomous AI

## Tier 1: Strongest Fit

### 1. NIST Measurement Science & Engineering (MSE) Grants
- **Solicitation**: 2025-NIST-MSE-01 via Grants.gov
- **Funding**: $10K-$500K/yr, up to 5 years
- **Deadline**: Rolling
- **Why**: Near-unique match. The project applies GUM - a NIST co-authored standard - to AI trustworthiness. No other AI safety proposal likely frames the problem as metrology. NIST's AI measurement science priorities and Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) align directly.

### 2. NSF Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) - NSF 25-543
- **Funding**: Up to $600K (Small) or $1.2M (Medium), 2-3 years
- **Deadline**: Rolling annual window
- **Why**: Solicitation explicitly calls for "safe learning and adaptive systems," "formal methods for verification and assurance of cyber-physical systems, including those that integrate AI," and "control and dynamics of complex cyber-physical systems." All three pillars map directly. Telecom is a legitimate CPS domain.

- **Funding**: Up to $2M over 24 months
- **Deadline**: ~~Abstracts March 2, 2026; full proposals April 17, 2026 (Amendment 01)~~
- **Status**: **Skipped (2026-04-01).** Timeline unfeasible for full proposal. Abstract deadline was missed (strongly encouraged, not required); 16 days was insufficient for 35-page proposal + cost workbook + BAA portal registration + UA Sponsored Projects engagement.
- **Why it was a strong fit**: Sought "high assurance, broadly applicable AI systems of systems" with provable compositional properties. GUM-based uncertainty quantification and SPC control charts provide exactly the "highly transparent composition" with provable properties that the program wanted.

### 4. NSF Safe Learning-Enabled Systems - NSF 23-562
- **Funding**: ~$1M-$2M per project, 3-4 years (partnership with Open Philanthropy and Good Ventures)
- **Deadline**: Watch for renewed solicitation
- **Why**: Demands "systems that are robust to extreme events and monitoring them for anomalous and unsafe behavior" with "precise mathematical and empirical safety guarantees and constraints." SPC-based thresholds and GUM uncertainty budgets deliver exactly this.

### 5. NSF SaTC 2.0 (Security, Privacy, and Trust in Cyberspace) - NSF 25-515
- **Funding**: Up to $1.2M over 4 years
- **Deadline**: Last Monday of September (annually) and last Monday of January (annually)
- **Why**: "Trust" is the core tenet. The project's central thesis - that trust can be metrologically measured and formally underwritten - speaks directly to the program. The enforcement architecture is a trust enforcement mechanism.

## Tier 2: Strong Fit

### 6. NSF AIMing (AI, Formal Methods, and Mathematical Reasoning) - NSF 24-554
- **Funding**: ~$600K-$1M (6-10 awards from ~$6M annual pool)
- **Deadline**: February 5, 2026 (annually)
- **Why**: Supports research at the intersection of AI and formal/mathematical reasoning. The metrological framework (GUM-based uncertainty propagation) is fundamentally a mathematical reasoning approach applied to AI systems. SPC control charts are formal statistical methods for verification.

### 7. NSF CISE Future Computing Research (Future CoRe) - NSF 25-543
- **Funding**: Up to $1M over 4 years
- **Deadline**: September 11, 2025 and February 5, 2026
- **Why**: CISE priority areas include AI and Cybersecurity. The telecom/networking application domain falls squarely within CISE's networking research portfolio.

### 8. ONR Long Range BAA - BAA N00014-25-S-B001
- **Funding**: Variable (hundreds of thousands to millions)
- **Deadline**: Rolling until September 30, 2026
- **Why**: Funds research on trustworthy AI/ML for defense applications. Autonomous naval systems require the kind of metrological trust assurance this project proposes. Bio-inspired regulation models have direct analogs in naval autonomous operations.

### 9. ARL BAA (Army Research Laboratory) - W911NF-23-S-0001-0002
- **Funding**: ~$1.5M for 5-year cooperative agreements
- **Deadline**: Rolling until November 20, 2027
- **Why**: Funds research on human-agent trust, autonomous systems teaming, and AI assurance. The metrological trust measurement feeds into their Collective Judgment Formation research on human-AI decision making.

### 10. AFOSR BAA - FA9550-25-S-0001
- **Funding**: Variable
- **Deadline**: Rolling
- **Why**: Covers trust and evaluation in autonomous systems, information and networks, and dynamic systems and control theory. All three pillars relevant. Circuit breaker control dynamics fit the dynamic systems portfolio; bio-inspired models align with nature-inspired computing interests.

### 11. NSF ReDDDoT (Responsible Design, Development, and Deployment of Technologies)
- **Funding**: $75K to $1.5M per award
- **Deadline**: July 8, 2025 (FY25 cycle)
- **Why**: FY25 explicitly focuses on AI. Wants "ethical, legal, community and societal considerations embedded in the lifecycle of technology's creation and use." The metrological trust framework provides a quantitative basis for responsible AI deployment.

### 12. ONR Young Investigator Program (YIP) FY2026
- **Funding**: $750K over 3 years
- **Deadline**: August 1, 2025
- **Why**: Autonomous systems trust is a priority area. Note: requires tenure-track or equivalent faculty position.

### 13. AFOSR Young Investigator Program (YIP) FY2026 - AFRL/AFOSR-2025-0006
- **Funding**: $450K over 3 years
- **Deadline**: September 19, 2025
- **Why**: Has funded trust in autonomous systems research. Note: PhD must have been received on or after April 1, 2018; requires faculty position.
