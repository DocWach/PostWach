## (a) CEDE List

The following results are fully owned by prior art. WySE must cede these cleanly.

**C1 — Giry monad on Meas (Giry 1982).**
The functor G: Meas -> Meas assigning to (X, F) the space of probability measures PX with sigma-algebra PF generated by evaluation maps ev_A(p) = p(A) is a monad. Its unit is the Dirac delta; its multiplication integrates. The Kleisli category Stoch has measurable spaces as objects and Markov kernels k: X -> GY as morphisms. This is the categorical semantics of a stochastic system over a continuous/measurable state space. [VERIFY: giry1982categorical]

**C2 — Bisimulation for labelled Markov processes (Desharnais, Edalat, Panangaden 2002).**
Generalizes probabilistic bisimulation (Larsen-Skou) to analytic (Polish) state spaces via sub-sigma-algebras. Two states are bisimilar iff they agree on all measurable sets in every invariant sub-sigma-algebra. The bisimulation relation is characterized by closed sets in the weak topology. The notion is definitionally sigma-algebra-relative: bisimilarity is a property of which sub-sigma-algebras are preserved, not a scalar. [VERIFY: desharnais2002bisim]

**C3 — Metrics for labelled Markov processes (Desharnais, Gupta, Jagadeesan, Panangaden 2004).**
Lifts the binary bisimulation to a pseudometric d(s, t) on analytic state spaces. The metric is a fixed-point of a functional on pseudometric spaces using the Kantorovich / Hutchinson metric on distributions. Zero distance iff bisimilar. This is the first continuous metrization of behavioral distance. [VERIFY: desharnais2004metrics]

**C4 — Bisimulation metrics for continuous MDPs (Ferns, Panangaden, Precup 2011).**
Extends to MDPs with continuous state spaces (e.g., robot navigation). Metric is defined recursively: d(s, t) = (1-c) * |R(s) - R(t)| + c * W_1(T(s,-), T(t,-)) where W_1 is the 1-Wasserstein (Kantorovich) distance between transition kernels and c in (0,1) is a discount. Proved to be a bisimulation metric (zero iff bisimilar under the continuous extension). Metrizes behavioral/reward distance. [VERIFY: ferns2011bisim]

**C5 — Coalgebraic behavioral metrics (Baldan, Bonchi, Kerstan, König 2018).**
Provides a general framework: given a functor H lifting to pseudometric spaces, every final H-coalgebra acquires a canonical behavioral distance. For the distribution functor (Giry monad), this recovers Kantorovich/Wasserstein. The metric is defined up to the choice of functor and lifting; the partition structure (which states are behaviorally equivalent) is derived, not independently specified. [VERIFY: baldan2018coalg]

**C6 — Weak bisimulations for the Giry monad (Doberkat 2010).**
Studies bisimulations for Kleisli morphisms of the Giry monad over Polish spaces. Reduces to bisimulations in the base category of stochastic relations. This is a behavioral equivalence on Markov kernels, not a metrization of partition structure. [VERIFY: doberkat2010weak]

**C7 — Kemeny-Snell strong lumpability (Kemeny and Snell 1976, finite case; Rosenblatt continuous extension).**
Strong lumpability for finite chains: a partition {S_1,...,S_k} of state space is strongly lumpable iff for all i, j, the sum sum_{s' in S_j} p(s, s') is the same for every s in S_i. Extended by Rosenblatt to continuous-valued Markov processes. The continuous analog is: a measurable partition (equivalently, a sub-sigma-algebra F_0 subset F) yields a lumped Markov kernel iff the original kernel is measurable with respect to the coarsened sigma-algebra in the appropriate disintegration sense. This is an EXACTNESS condition on the partition, not a distance. [VERIFY: kemeny1976finite]

## (b) Candidate DELTA

### The continuous generalization of D_s

In the finite case, D_s = (1/|S_B|) * sum_{y in S_B} 1/|m^{-1}(y)| records the average reciprocal preimage cardinality of a surjective lumping map m: S_A -> S_B. The finite structural record is the triple (K1, K2, K3) = (lumping map m, partition P = {m^{-1}(y) : y in S_B}, preimage sizes {|m^{-1}(y)|}). The GAP established in the finite case is: LumpSTD / Kr-Kp / bisimulation metrics / lax morphisms all conflate morphisms that differ only in K1-K3.

**Generalization to measurable spaces.** Let (X, F) and (Y, G) be measurable spaces and let q: X -> GY be a Markov kernel (a Kleisli morphism in Stoch). The continuous analog of a deterministic lumping map m: X -> Y is a measurable surjection f: (X, F) -> (Y, G); the deterministic kernel q_f(B | x) = 1_{f(x) in B} is the degenerate case. For a general (non-deterministic) lumping, the structural record must be lifted to the sigma-algebra level.

**Continuous structural record D_s^cont = (K1^c, K2^c, K3^c):**

- K1^c: the lumping kernel q: X -> GY (the stochastic morphism itself, a Markov kernel).
- K2^c: the lumping partition structure, now encoded as a sub-sigma-algebra F_0 = q^{-1}(G) = {q^{-1}(B) : B in G} subset F. This is the coarsening of F induced by the lumping; it records which events in X are distinguishable by the lumped system.
- K3^c: the "fiber measure" of the lumping. For the deterministic case, this is the cardinality of the preimage fiber. In the measurable case, this is the conditional disintegration of the marginal measure mu_X with respect to the sub-sigma-algebra F_0 = q^{-1}(G): for each y in Y, the disintegration kernel mu_{X|f^{-1}(y)} records the shape of the preimage fiber as a probability measure, not just its cardinality. The continuous analog of 1/|m^{-1}(y)| is the entropy or width of this disintegration measure: a fiber concentrated on a single point is "exact" (information-preserving), a diffuse fiber is "lossy."

**Formal claim (candidate, not yet theorem).** Two stochastic morphisms q, q': X -> GY that are behaviorally equivalent under every measure on Y (i.e., have the same pushforward: q_* mu = q'_* mu for all mu on (X, F)) can still differ in (K2^c, K3^c): they can induce different sub-sigma-algebras F_0 != F_0' (different partition structures on X) and different fiber disintegrations. The continuous bisimulation metrics (C3, C4) metrize the behavioral distance d(q, q') on Y via Wasserstein but do not distinguish q from q' when d = 0. The structural record (K2^c, K3^c) is invisible to the Wasserstein metric.

### The structural axis remains un-metrized in the continuous case

The prior art (C2-C6) metrizes BEHAVIORAL distance: how differently two systems behave when observed through Y. None of C2-C6 assigns a scalar to the partition structure on X or the fiber geometry of the lumping map. Specifically:

- C2 (binary bisimulation): determines whether F_0 is sufficient for the dynamics but does not score the coarseness of F_0 or the fiber distribution.
- C3-C4 (metric bisimulation): assigns a distance d(s, t) to pairs of SOURCE states based on behavioral output. The partition map f (or kernel q) itself is not scored; two different lumpings that produce the same behavioral distance are metrically identical.
- C5 (coalgebraic): the final coalgebra distance recovers behavioral equivalence classes; the structural decomposition of X (how many states merge, how diffuse the fiber is) is not captured.
- C6 (Giry monad bisimulation): works on Kleisli morphisms; equates morphisms that agree on measurable events, not on fiber geometry.

**The gap is the same in the continuous case.** The structural axis of the (D_s, D_b) plane is still unmetrized. Behavioral metrics score D_b (output/reward proximity); no existing metric scores D_s^cont (partition coarseness + fiber diffuseness) independently.

### What a finite/symbolic witness shows

A concrete witness demonstrating the gap in the continuous case can be constructed symbolically as follows (analogous to the finite counterexample in the RBW review):

Let X = [0, 1] with Lebesgue sigma-algebra F, Y = {0, 1} with G = power set. Define two surjections:
- f_alpha: f_alpha^{-1}(0) = {0} (a single point), f_alpha^{-1}(1) = (0, 1] (Lebesgue measure 1).
- f_beta: f_beta^{-1}(0) = [0, 1/2], f_beta^{-1}(1) = (1/2, 1] (each fiber has Lebesgue measure 1/2).

Both f_alpha and f_beta are measurable surjections X -> Y. If the stochastic dynamics on X are uniform Lebesgue, the pushforward distribution on Y is Bernoulli(1/2) under both maps (assuming Lebesgue(0) = 0 and Lebesgue((0,1]) = 1). The behavioral distance d_Wasserstein(f_alpha, f_beta) = 0 under any measure that assigns measure zero to the single point {0}.

Yet:
- K2^c under f_alpha: the sub-sigma-algebra F_0^alpha = {{}, {0}, (0,1], [0,1]}. The fiber {0} has Lebesgue measure 0 (a point mass; maximum resolution in that region) while fiber (0,1] has Lebesgue measure 1 (maximum diffusion). K3^c records extreme non-uniformity.
- K2^c under f_beta: the sub-sigma-algebra F_0^beta = {{}, [0,1/2], (1/2,1], [0,1]}. Both fibers have equal Lebesgue measure 1/2. K3^c records uniform compression.

The continuous analog of D_s:
- D_s^cont(f_alpha) reflects a near-singular (high-fidelity at {0}) plus diffuse (lossy on (0,1]): information-geometrically the two fibers have very different widths.
- D_s^cont(f_beta) reflects uniform compression: both fibers have equal measure width.

A verification engineer who needs to know whether critical states (near x = 0) are preserved with high fidelity would correctly rank f_alpha above f_beta on the structural axis; behavioral metrics (Wasserstein) cannot distinguish them.

### Sub-sigma-algebra formulation

The natural continuous analog of K2 (the partition) is the sub-sigma-algebra F_0 = q^{-1}(G) induced by the lumping. The natural analog of K3 (preimage sizes) is the collection of fiber measures {nu_{x, q} : x in X} from the disintegration theorem: mu_X = integral nu_{f^{-1}(y)} d(f_* mu_X)(y). A scalar D_s^cont can be constructed as the average (over Y) of a scalar width measure on the fiber measure nu_{f^{-1}(y)}, for example the differential entropy H(nu_{f^{-1}(y)}) = - integral log(dnu/dlambda) dnu for absolutely continuous fibers, or a Renyi entropy, or simply the support diameter. Exact lumping (K-S strong lumpability) corresponds to Dirac fiber measures (zero entropy, zero width). Diffuse lumping corresponds to high-entropy fibers.

This construction generalizes D_s from cardinality to measure-theoretic fiber width. The GAP-GIRY delta is: this scalar (or its record form) is well-defined on measurable lumpings over continuous state spaces and is not computed by any of C2-C6.

### WySE delta in the continuous case

The genuine delta is:

1. **The record triple lifts to measurable spaces.** K1^c = Markov kernel, K2^c = induced sub-sigma-algebra, K3^c = fiber disintegration collection. The record separates behaviorally equivalent morphisms that differ in their sigma-algebra coarsening.
2. **D_s^cont is a well-defined scalar on the structural axis.** It requires choosing a fiber-width scalar (entropy, support size, Renyi); each choice yields a valid structural degree on measurable lumpings.
3. **The fiber-width formulation is WySE-specific.** Prior art defines bisimulation (binary structural equivalence) and behavioral metrics (Wasserstein on outputs). No prior work defines a scalar structural degree on the fiber measure that is independent of the behavioral output distance. This is the content of GAP-GIRY.
4. **The institutional framing applies.** In the 3/2-institution U, the satisfaction condition carries both D_s and D_b. In the continuous extension, D_s^cont is parameterized by the sub-sigma-algebra F_0 and the fiber measure collection; D_b^cont is the Wasserstein metric on the pushforward. The lax satisfaction condition from the RBW review (T-UNI) applies: reducing a signature changes F_0 and therefore changes D_s^cont, with an explicit information-loss obstruction term.
5. **The +probability thread (TMS ch22).** Zeigler's stochastic homomorphism (TMS Thm 22.1) is the exact-lumping case: it requires the commuting diagram to hold precisely, which corresponds to Dirac fiber measures (K3^c = zero entropy). The approximate stochastic homomorphism (TMS §22.5, the (E, D)-space / lumpability zone) introduces a tolerance epsilon, but does not independently score the partition structure K2^c or fiber width K3^c within that zone. WySE's D_s^cont does.

## (d) Open Questions

**OQ1 — Which fiber-width scalar is canonical?**
Differential entropy, Renyi-2, Wasserstein diameter of the fiber, and conditional mutual information I(X; f(X)) are all valid candidates for K3^c. Each yields a different D_s^cont. The choice affects the WySE verification claim. A principled selection (possibly: Renyi-2 for its connection to KL divergence and for behaving nicely under Gaussian fibers) should be argued in the next round.

**OQ2 — Does the lax institution (T-UNI) carry forward to the continuous case?**
The RBW review established that signature reducts change D_s in the finite case, requiring the lax quantitative institution (obstructed satisfaction condition). In the continuous case, reducing a signature corresponds to coarsening G (the sigma-algebra on Y), which further coarsens F_0 = q^{-1}(G) and changes the fiber disintegration. The lax satisfaction condition should still hold, but the explicit form of the obstruction term (information loss under sigma-algebra coarsening) needs to be characterized. Likely involves the relative entropy D_KL(nu_{F_0} || nu_{F_0'}).

**OQ3 — Relationship to the Kemeny-Snell continuous extension.**
Rosenblatt's continuous lumpability condition is an exactness criterion (zero-entropy fibers). How does D_s^cont refine the Kemeny-Snell condition in the approximate/non-exact case? The approximate morphism tolerance epsilon (from TMS §22.5) should correspond to D_s^cont being bounded away from 1 (exact). Making this correspondence precise closes the loop from TMS ch22 to the institution framework.

**OQ4 — The coinduction / fixed-point characterization.**
The DEP bisimulation metric (C3-C4) is defined as the greatest fixed point of a contractive functional (Banach). Does D_s^cont have a fixed-point characterization, or is it a one-shot measurement of the fiber geometry? The behavioral metric is naturally coinductive; the structural degree is naturally inductive (it measures the lumping map, not the dynamics). This distinction is load-bearing for the proof structure.

**OQ5 — Computability on continuous spaces.**
In the finite case, D_s is computable: it is a ratio of integers. For D_s^cont, computation requires evaluating a fiber disintegration, which in general requires knowledge of the marginal measure and the lumping kernel. For the verification use case (where we have a concrete software model), the state spaces are either finite or finitely-parameterized (hybrid systems); the question is whether D_s^cont can be evaluated from the DEVS-level signature without requiring an explicit measure over the continuous state space.

**OQ6 — Connection to enriched institutions and Lawvere's [0,inf]-enriched categories.**
The memory note on the WySE category-theory thread (project_wyse_category_theory_thread.md) places D_b in a Lawvere [0,inf]-enriched category. D_s^cont, once assigned a scalar via fiber entropy, may also sit in such a category: morphisms between lumpings ordered by fiber width. An enriched institution where both (D_s^cont, D_b) are enriched-category-valued would be the CT-native formulation of the unified object U in the continuous case.
